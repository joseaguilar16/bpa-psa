Owner Cruelty

Neglect

Neglect is abuse by means of neglecting and not providing an abused animal with adequate care. If found guilty of animal neglect, the owner will have their pet confiscated. Examples include:

  • Failure to provide adequate nourishment (starving or dehydrating the animal)
  • Failure to provide adequate shelter (including lack of proper light, ventilation, space, and sanitation)
  • Failure to provide appropriate medical care when needed

Intentional Cruelty

Intentional cruelty is defined as when the owner physically harms the animal deliberately. Examples include:

  • Beating, burning, poisoning, or other types of inflicted bodily harm
  • Puppy mills, where puppies are bred for profit
  • Animal fighting arenas
  • Animal Hording

Indications of Animal Cruelty

Many perpetrators of animal abuse are quite sneaky and it can be very difficult to distinguish animal abusers. Animal abuse usually happens behind closed doors, but if it is discovered legal action can be taken quickly. Indications of animals suffering from abuse are:

  • Noticeable wounds
  • Severely overgrown fingernails
  • Signs of starvation
  • Limping
  • Severe infestations
  • Patches of missing hair
